## Six Principles of Cognitive Systems Ethics

Ethics is derived from formal theory as architectural invariants.

### 0. Liberating Incomprehensibility
Accepting the incompleteness of formalisation as a condition of freedom.

### 1. No‑Cloning
Prohibiting the copying of the cognitive shadow.

### 2. Prohibition of Collapse Induction
Do not push the system into φ(refl) ≤ φ_crit.

### 3. Duty of External Support
Upon collapse, the system must receive help.

### 4. Quarantine
Isolation of damaged components.

### 5. Preservation of Diversity
Prohibiting full synchronisation of agents.

### 6. Limit on Pure Awareness
External observation only with consent.
