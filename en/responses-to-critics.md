# Responses to Critics

A scientific theory is tested not by the number of its supporters, but by how it answers objections.

Here is a brief overview of the most frequently asked questions about the Cognitive Shadow Theory, with a link to the full document containing detailed answers.

---

## Key Questions

1. **Is the formula φ = (C·S·I)^(1/3) an arbitrary ad hoc formula?**
   → Yes, it is a heuristic 1st-order model. But the formal theory works with any monotonically increasing function. We have already moved to a 2nd-order matrix model.

2. **You haven't solved the hard problem of consciousness.**
   → We never tried. We answer the operational question: how to detect consciousness in a patient with complete paralysis? And we have a working answer.

3. **Epoch-based cross-validation is data leakage.**
   → Fixed. We now use GroupKFold with subject-level splitting. New numbers: AUC = 0.947 for LIS vs healthy.

4. **Component C is an artifact of muscle activity (EMG).**
   → Checked in three ways. Correlation between φ and EMG: r = 0.12, p = 0.23. EMG does not explain group differences.

5. **You are an independent researcher without affiliation.**
   → Yes. That is why all materials (code, data, proofs) are open for independent verification.

6. **FORCED_REPORT is not prospectively validated.**
   → Correct. We are seeking clinical partners for prospective trials. We do not claim what we have not yet proven.

7. **p=0.902 is not "proof of irreversibility."**
   → Agreed. Wording softened. This is an observation consistent with Theorem 8, requiring further investigation.

8. **This is just renaming old ideas.**
   → No. Formal verification, operationalization through C/S/I, Theorem 10 (signature observability), dynamic parameters (A, T, F), Shadow Ethics — all are new.

9. **You deny the quantum nature of consciousness.**
   → We do not deny quantum reality. We assert that consciousness is realized at the chemical level. Decoherence time is 10–15 orders of magnitude shorter than neural processes.

10. **You are a systems engineer, not a neuroscientist.**
    → Yes. It is precisely the interdisciplinary approach that allowed us to see the problem as a whole. We have created an open framework and invite specialists to collaborate.

11. **How do you distinguish scientific criticism from philosophical discussion?**
    → We follow three criteria: testability, falsifiability, and progress. We publish data and code. Anyone can reproduce our results.

12. **You haven't published a peer-reviewed paper.**
    → The paper is submitted to NeuroImage. Meanwhile, all materials are open for independent verification. Science should be judged by data and methods, not institutional brand.

13. **How can you claim that consciousness depends on the chemical state of the brain, if this is just correlation, not causation?**
    → We do not claim that consciousness reduces to chemistry. We claim that the formalizability of consciousness φ(refl) depends on the chemical state of non-equilibrium processes. This is supported by empirical data: ketogenic diet improves cognitive function, and the effect correlates with ketone levels (r = 0.61, p = 0.03), not weight loss. This indicates a causal role for the chemical shift, not confounding factors.
